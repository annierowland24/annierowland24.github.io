As you’ve seen by the task, the smallest amount of information you share online could be connected together, allowing your password to be easy to figure out. This is similar to using a weak password as a part of your credentials to any site opens you up to a wide variety of issues. These include:

  • They are easy to hack - this could allow you to be a victim of a cyber attack, as your password would be easy to obtain.

  • Easy to guess - using common passwords such as 123 or abc are easily guessed by anyone, regardless of their technical background. It is a risk to use simple and common passwords as it significantly weakens your security.

  • Using weak passwords can lead to financial loss as the attackers can access your banking accounts with the information they have found. It can also lead to your private data being exposed across the internet, once your details have been found they could be shared.

    • Safe solutions:

    It’s a very common mistake to use the same password for several different websites. However secure you believe this password is, it only takes the hacker to access one of your passwords across the different sites and gain access to the rest. You should ensure you use a different password for each site, and each password should contain:

  • Lower and uppercase letters.

  • Punctuation and numbers.

  • Numbers and words not specifically relevant to you, so your social media cannot be connected to it.

  • At least 8-12 characters.

    • In order to keep your security at the maximum level, you should also invoke multi factor authentication. This will allow you to be notified when someone is trying to log into your account, as well as the log in having to pass more than one stage to gain access to the account.
